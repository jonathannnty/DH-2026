If you are taking an Uber to our event, you can set your destination to the Computer Science and Engineering Building (3235 Voigt Dr, La Jolla, CA 92093) and ask them to drop you off along Voigt Drive at the bus stop next to the crosswalk (labeled with red dots in the image below).

Public Transportation

La Jolla has a great public transportation system that is accessible through the PRONTO app or tap-to-pay. More details for buses and the trolley (including payment, which is about $2.50 per one-way 2-hour trip) are available on the SDMTS website.
For those arriving through the San Diego airport, there is a free electric shuttle bus (the San Diego Flyer) that can take you from the airport to the Old Town Transit Center (4005 Taylor Street, San Diego, CA 92110). The shuttle arrives every 20-30 minutes and stops at both Terminal 1 and Terminal 2.
From there, you can take the Blue Line trolley directly to the UCSD Central Campus Station. There are many ways to pay for the trolley, including contactless payment and the PRONTO app.
You can find more information on the SDMTS website.

FlixBus

FlixBus covers many locations in California, including Los Angeles, San Diego, San Francisco, Sacramento, Bakersfield, Fresno, and Stockton, and can take you directly to the UC San Diego campus, at Campus Point Drive immediately south of the UC San Diego Health La Jolla Blue Line Trolley Station. You can find more information on the FlixBus website.

Eligibility Criteria + Rules ​

Participants must be a student at any accredited university/college.
Participants must be registered beforehand (diamondhacks.acmucsd.com).
Teams can have at most 4 participants. Furthermore, team and project names must be appropriate.
Participants can NOT be a part of multiple teams.
All materials submitted should be created during the allotted time frame of the hackathon.
Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.
Participants must abide by fair play. (e.g. No sabotaging other teams, manipulating results, or any other violation of the aforementioned guidelines.)
Overall, be courteous to people!
Participants must attend both the opening and closing ceremonies to be eligible for prizes.
See the MLH Code of Conduct for more information. Failure to follow the guidelines outlined above can result in disqualification. If you notice any of the rules being violated, please talk to a volunteer in a maroon DiamondHacks shirt or reach out to us at hackathon@acmucsd.org.

Tracks ​

We have 5 tracks that you can submit project to. Any project can be submitted to up to one track, and aligning your project with a track isn’t mandatory. However, the tracks can help you think of project ideas, and we have prizes for each track!
Alchemy of the Earth (Social Impact/Sustainability) Prize: Owala 40 oz Bundle
Harness the ancient powers of nature to protect our realm. In this track, we challenge you to brew solutions for the world’s most pressing environmental and social issues. Whether you’re crafting code to heal the planet, combat climate change, or empower underserved communities, use your “green magic” to ensure a sustainable future for all.
Elixirs of Vitality (Health) Prize: Peak Design Tech Pouch
Step into the infirmary of the future! This track is dedicated to the magic of healing and the science of well-being. We’re looking for enchantments that improve physical health, mental clarity, or medical accessibility. From “charms” that monitor vitals to restorative platforms that offer aid to the weary, use your craft to preserve the most precious magic of all: life.
The Scholar’s Spellbook (Education) Prize: Mini Projector
Knowledge is the ultimate source of power. For this track, reimagine the way wisdom is shared and spells are learned. Create immersive platforms, interactive scrolls, or gamified tutorials that turn complex subjects into intuitive mastery. Unlock the gates of the Great Geisel Library and make education an enchanting experience for every aspiring wizard.
Enchanted Commerce (Commerce/E-Commerce) Prize: Apple AirTags
Transform the marketplace into a realm of wonder! This track challenges you to revolutionize how we exchange treasures and manage our gold. Develop seamless payment portals, immersive virtual storefronts, or clever logistical charms that make trade effortless. Turn the mundane act of shopping into a legendary quest for the perfect find.
The Rogue’s Ritual (Wildcard) Prize: Sony SRS-XB100 Speaker
Not all magic can be contained with a textbook or a single school of thought. This track is for the anomalies, the experimentalists, and the boundary-breakers. If your project defies the known laws of alchemy, health, education, or commerce, bring your most daring enchantments here. Whether it’s a revolutionary tool, a chaotic masterpiece, or a spell that creates an entirely new category of wonder, prove that the most powerful magic is the kind that refuses to be defined.

Project Submission ​

Projects are due Sunday, April 5th, at 12:30PM. In addition to submitting your code and any other necessary files, you must also include a 3 minute video explaining your project. Submissions must be in English and uploaded on Devpost.

Judging ​

Projects will be judged in four main criteria: Idea, Experience, Implementation, and Demo/Presentation. After the project submission deadline, participants will showcase their projects in a science-fair style format, where judges will visit each presentation. More details will be announced on Discord closer to the deadline.

House Cup ​

Compete for glory and the ultimate bragging rights! You can earn points for your House simply by attending events, workshops, and activities throughout DiamondHacks. Just get your QR code scanned when you check in to an event to secure your points. The House that gathers the most points by the end of the weekend will unlock a special reward for every single member! Prize: Mystery Prize for all members of the winning House
